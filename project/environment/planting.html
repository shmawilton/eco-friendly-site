Importance of Planting

carbon dioxide levels

Trees absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen through photosynthesis, helping to balance the atmosphere.

Pollution

Trees absorb pollutants from the air and filter it, helping to improve air quality.

Soil Erosion

Trees help to stabilize the soil with their roots, reducing the risk of erosion.

Wildlife Habitat

Trees provide food, shelter, and nesting sites for various species of animals, helping to preserve biodiversity.

Planting Seedlings

Suitable location

Choose a location that has well-draining soil, adequate sunlight, and enough space for the tree to grow to its full size.

Prepare the soil

Dig a hole that is twice as wide as the root ball and just as deep. Loosen the soil at the bottom of the hole to allow the roots to spread out easily.

Water the seedling

Soak the root ball of the seedling in water for a few hours to help hydrate the roots and reduce transplant shock.

Plant the seedling

Place the seedling in the hole so that the top of the root ball is level with the surrounding soil. Fill the hole with soil, tamping it down gently around the roots to remove any air pockets.

Water the seedling again

Water the seedling thoroughly to settle the soil and encourage root growth.

Mulch the soil

Spread a layer of mulch around the base of the seedling, keeping it away from the trunk. Mulch helps retain moisture and control weeds.

Monitor the seedling

Keep an eye on the seedling and water it regularly, especially during dry periods, to ensure that it stays hydrated and healthy.
